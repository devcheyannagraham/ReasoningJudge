2. **Defining Variables**: Let \( x \) represent the cost of the ball. According to the question, the cost of the bat will then be \( x + 1.00 \). This definition is crucial because it allows us to translate the verbal problem into a mathematical equation.

3. **Formulating the Equation**: Combining the costs of the bat and the ball, we can set up the equation: 
   - \( x + (x + 1.00) = 1.10 \). 
   Here, we are summing the cost of the ball and the cost of the bat, equating it to the total cost.

4. **Simplifying the Equation**: When we simplify this equation: 
   - \( 2x + 1.00 = 1.10 \). 
   This combining of like terms reveals that the equation represents two times the ball's cost, along with the additional cost of the bat.

5. **Isolating the Variable**: Next, we subtract $1.00 from both sides to isolate the variable related to the ball:
   - \( 2x = 0.10 \). 
   This step is pivotal because it helps us focus solely on the term representing the ball's cost.

6. **Calculating the Cost**: Dividing both sides by 2 gives us:
   - \( x = 0.05 \). 
   This solution indicates that the ball costs $0.05.

However, given the total of $1.10, this does not satisfy all conditions, as it leads to the bat costing $1.10, which exceeds the total. Instead, realizing that we must formulate an equation based on both costs together helps clarify the solution correctly.
